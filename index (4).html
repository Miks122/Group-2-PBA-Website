Introduction

This webpage explains our Root Cause Analysis (RCA) about the issue of Slow Internet in school and at home. We used the 5 Whys method to understand why slow internet happens and how we can help solve it. Since the internet is important for learning, this issue can affect students schoolwork and online schoolwork. As Grade 8 students, we analyzed the problem in a simple and clear way and shared some solutions so we could help improve internet connection.

Main Problem

Many students experience Slow Internet when doing online tasks, research, or submitting activities, which makes studying more difficult. Because of poor connection, pages load slowly, files fail to upload, and online classes are interrupted. This causes delays, stress, and difficulty finishing school requirements on time, especially when deadlines are strict. As a result, students may feel frustrated, lose focus, and struggle to perform their best in school.

Root Cause Analysis Using 5 Whys

Here is the 5 Whys breakdown to understand the real reason why slow internet happens:

1st Why: Why is the internet slow?
➤ Several Factors affect Internet Speed, such as the quality of the Service Provider, the distance between the device, the amount of devices connected to the router and the router and the performance of the router itself.
2nd Why: Why does it lag or load slowly?
➤ Because the router is handling too much devices connected to it, Many connected devices may be overwhelming and can cause overheating, as these devices may be doing heavy network activities such as downloading large files, streaming movies or installing games, which can slow down the network for everyone.
3rd Why: Why are many devices connected?
➤ Because only one router is being used to support all the devices and buying more routers is expensive, as a result, all users rely on a single router, causing heavy lag or slowness.
4th Why: Why cant the Internet handle it all?
➤ Because the internet plan does not provide enough speed or strength to support the connected devices all simultaneously connected to the router.
5th Why: Why wasn’t the Internet plan based on usage needs?
➤ Because the internet plan is selected based off on budget rather than how many devices and applications require a dependable connection for each of the devices.
Root Cause: The root cause of the slow internet is the router or the internet being overwhelmed or overworked or overheated by too much devices sharing it and an internet plan that isnt strong enough or fast enough to be reliable for these devices, combined with a poor quality router, distance from the device to the router and the Service provider and more.

Identified Root Cause

Many students experience slow internet when doing online tasks, researching, or submitting activities, and this makes studying more difficult. With a weak connection, pages take a long time to load, files sometimes fail to upload, and online classes get interrupted or lag during lessons. These problems cause delays, stress, and difficulty finishing school requirements on time, especially when deadlines are strict. Because of this, students may feel frustrated, lose focus, and struggle to perform at their best in school. After analyzing the situation, we found that the main reason for slow internet is that the internet plan being used is not fast enough for the number of devices connected. When many people in the household are online at the same time, the connection becomes crowded and the speed is divided, leading to slow browsing and unstable internet.

Proposed Solutions and Preventive Measures

  • Upgrade to a better internet plan with higher speed and larger strength to support multiple devices and give a dependable connection for it.
  • Limit the number of connected devices during important events such as online classes or doing homework to reduce the lag during these times.
  • Place the router in an open and larged space area for it to have a large coverage among all the devices and to improve signal strength.
  • Do regular router resets to refresh the connection and to refresh the amount of the devices connected to the router.
  • Use an Ethernet cable for devices that require uninterupted, stable and depenendable internet connections during important events such as studying.
