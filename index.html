Slow Internet Analysis

Root Cause Analysis Project - Group 2, Grade 8 TLE

üè† Project Introduction

Slow internet makes it hard for Grade 8 students to do online classes and homework. Our Group 2 used the 5 Whys method - we kept asking "Why?" five times to find the real reason for slow internet.

We checked internet in our homes, tested speeds, asked classmates and parents questions, and found out what makes internet slow during study time. This project shows what we found and easy fixes for families.

‚ö†Ô∏è Main Problems

These are the biggest internet problems Grade 8 students face every day:

üì± Websites Load Very Slowly
Google Classroom, DepEd websites, and research pages take too long to open. Students wait and wait for pages to load while trying to do research or check assignments. This makes studying take much longer than it should.
üì§ File Upload Failures
Google Drive files, homework assignments, and project submissions take forever to upload or just stop working completely. Students miss deadlines because their work won't send and teachers mark them late.
üé• Video Classes Keep Pausing
Zoom classes, recorded lessons, and educational YouTube videos keep stopping to buffer. Students miss what the teacher says and can't follow along with important lessons or instructions.
‚è∞ Late Homework & Stress
Because internet is so slow, students can't finish assignments or submit on time. They get low grades, parents get angry, and everyone feels stressed about schoolwork that won't upload.

üîç 5 Whys Root Cause Analysis

Our group used the 5 Whys method step by step to find the real cause:

‚ùì 1st Why: Too many phones, laptops, tablets and TVs all connected to the same router during study time when everyone is home.
‚ùì 2nd Why: Every house only has one Wi-Fi router for the whole family with no separate networks.
‚ùì 3rd Why: Cheap internet plans can't handle many devices using internet at once.
‚ùì 4th Why: Parents pick the cheapest internet plans instead of faster ones the family needs.
‚úÖ MAIN PROBLEM: Cheap slow internet + too many devices on one router = internet always slow at night

üí° Practical Solutions

Our group tested these solutions and they work! Here are the best fixes:

üöÄ Get Faster Internet
Change to faster internet plan. Gives enough speed for everyone at home.
üì¥ Turn Off Extra Devices
Turn OFF phones, games, and TVs during homework time. Keep only student laptop on.
üìç Move Router Better
Put router in middle of house, high up, away from walls and microwave.
üîÑ Restart Router
Unplug router for 30 seconds. Fixes most speed problems.
üîå Use Internet Cable
Plug laptop directly into router with cable. Gets full fast speed every time.
üì∂ Router Settings
Turn on setting to make student laptop faster than phones and TVs.

üìã Conclusion & Key Findings

We found that most slow internet happens because families get cheap internet but use too many phones, TVs, and laptops all at the same time at night.

The biggest problem is too many devices sharing slow internet. When everyone uses internet together, nobody gets good speed. That's why classes keep stopping!
